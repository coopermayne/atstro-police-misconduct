# Ryan Twyman - Case Notes

## Basic Info
- **Name**: Ryan Twyman
- **Age**: 24
- **Gender**: Male
- **Race**: Black
- **Date of Death**: June 6, 2019
- **Location**: E 132nd St and San Pedro St, Willowbrook, CA 90061
- **County**: Los Angeles
- **Agency**: Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department

## Officers Involved
- **Andrew Lyons** - Fired, charged, convicted. Pled no contest to assault with semiautomatic firearm and assault under color of authority. Sentenced to 30 days jail + 2 years probation. First LA County law enforcement conviction in a shooting in ~20 years.
- **Christopher Muse** - Not charged (struck by car door)

## Timeline
- **June 6, 2019**: Shooting occurs ~7:30 PM in apartment complex parking lot. Twyman sitting in Kia Forte when deputies approach. When Muse opens rear door, Twyman backs up, striking Muse with door. Both deputies fire. Lyons retrieves semiautomatic rifle and continues shooting after car stops. 34 rounds total discharged. Twyman killed, passenger Daimeon Leffall unharmed.
- **January 2020**: Sheriff Alex Villanueva announces one deputy fired, another suspended 30 days
- **November 2020**: LA County Board of Supervisors approves $3.9M settlement
- **March 2022**: Lyons formally charged with voluntary manslaughter and assault
- **February 2023**: Lyons ordered to stand trial
- **January 12, 2024**: Lyons pleads no contest to two assault charges (manslaughter dropped)

## Key Facts
- No weapons found in Twyman's car
- Twyman was a father of three
- Deputies allegedly violated LASD policy forbidding deadly force against people in vehicles
- Shooting sparked days of angry protests, national attention
- $3.9 million civil settlement to family (represented by Cochran Firm, Brian Dunn)
- Supervisor Mark Ridley-Thomas called it "a sobering reminder of the family's and community's needs for justice, healing and compassion"
