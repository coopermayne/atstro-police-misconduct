## Incident Overview

On November 8, 2022, a routine traffic stop by the California Highway Patrol escalated into a fatal shooting in Fontana. Robert Williams, 45, was shot by CHP officers after a pursuit and confrontation.

## Initial Stop

**8:15 PM** - CHP officer attempted to stop Williams' vehicle for expired registration on Interstate 10.

**8:17 PM** - Williams failed to yield, leading to a pursuit lasting approximately 8 minutes.

**8:25 PM** - Williams exited the freeway and stopped in a residential area of Fontana.

## Confrontation

When Williams exited his vehicle, officers observed a firearm. According to the CHP incident report, Williams raised the weapon toward officers. Four CHP officers discharged their firearms. Williams was struck multiple times.

## Response

Fontana Police Department arrived to assist with scene security. San Bernardino County Fire responded and transported Williams to Kaiser Permanente Fontana Medical Center, where he was pronounced dead.

## Investigation Findings

The San Bernardino County District Attorney's Office reviewed the case and declined to file charges, determining the officers' use of force was legally justified. Body camera footage from all four officers was reviewed.

**Key Findings:**
- Williams was found to possess a loaded .45 caliber handgun
- Toxicology revealed methamphetamine in Williams' system
- Officers gave multiple verbal commands before firing
- Williams' family disputes the DA's findings

## Civil Litigation

Williams' family filed a federal civil rights lawsuit in March 2023, alleging excessive force and inadequate de-escalation. The lawsuit is ongoing in U.S. District Court, Central District of California.

## Policy Implications

This case was cited in discussions about CHP pursuit policies and the need for mental health crisis intervention training. The CHP subsequently announced expanded training programs in 2023.

## Media Coverage

The incident received regional media coverage. Local activists organized a memorial and called for independent review of officer-involved shootings in San Bernardino County.
