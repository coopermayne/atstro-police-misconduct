## About Los Angeles County

Los Angeles County is the most populous county in the United States, home to over 10 million residents across 88 incorporated cities and numerous unincorporated areas. The county's law enforcement landscape is complex, involving multiple agencies with overlapping jurisdictions.

### Law Enforcement in LA County

**Primary Agencies:**
- **Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD)**: Serves unincorporated areas and 42 contract cities
- **Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD)**: Primary law enforcement for the City of Los Angeles
- **Long Beach Police Department**: Second-largest city in the county
- **Glendale Police Department**
- **Pasadena Police Department**
- Plus dozens of smaller municipal police departments

### Geographic Context

- **Total Area**: 4,753 square miles
- **Population**: ~10.1 million (2023 estimate)
- **Cities**: 88 incorporated municipalities
- **Unincorporated Areas**: Served directly by LASD

### Demographics

Los Angeles County is one of the most diverse regions in the world:
- No single ethnic majority
- Over 200 languages spoken
- Significant populations of Latino, Asian, Black, and White residents
- Wide socioeconomic diversity from Beverly Hills to areas with high poverty rates

### Historical Policing Issues

LA County has been at the center of numerous pivotal moments in American policing and civil rights:

- **1965**: Watts Riots following years of police-community tensions
- **1991**: Rodney King beating by LAPD officers
- **1992**: Los Angeles riots after officers acquitted in King beating
- **2000s**: Various corruption scandals including LAPD Rampart and LASD investigations
- **2020-present**: Ongoing scrutiny of deputy gangs within LASD

### Reform Efforts

Recent years have seen increased oversight and reform initiatives:
- Civilian oversight commissions for LAPD and LASD
- Body-worn camera programs
- Enhanced use-of-force policies
- Mental health crisis response teams
- Community policing initiatives
